This year, Pravega introduces a competition in theatrical performances among students all across the nation. The teams use theatrical performance as a medium of argument to speak for a cause they deem fit.
We present PROSCENIUM, a first of its kind.

×

Preliminary Video Auditions:

  1. The video is to be uploaded in a separate google drive folder (named after the team-name). The corresponding link should be submitted and the folder must be shared with sapman46@gmail.com, mainakguha19@gmail.com, and coccus4259@gmail.com at the least.
  2. Video and audio quality should be as clear as possible. Illegible audio may lead to disqualification. Video quality should not be below 720p.
    (Note: All movements and expressions should be clearly visible in the video.)
  3. A discussion about the act/script of the act may be uploaded along with the video in writing. The script should be either original or adapted. Plagiarism in script is strictly prohibited and if detected, will lead to immediate disqualification. Judges’ discretion on what should be considered as an “adaptation” or “plagiarism” would be final.
    The discussion should also make note of all elements (viz. poetry, songs etc.) that may have been used in the performance and are not original.
  4. The video should be single frame. No zooming/editing is allowed. Camera should be fixed at one place.
  5. Maximum video length limit is 15 minutes. Though a maximum of 5 minutes extra time may be considered in some cases for the sake of necessary artistic liberty, the discretion shall be the judges’.
  6. Subtitles might be provided for important dialogues in other languages. Usage of subtitles should be kept as “minimum” as possible.
  7. Usage of water, fire or anything that might harm the stage is prohibited. Nudity of primary/secondary sexual organs is strongly discouraged. Offensive usage in language and gestures is strongly discouraged.
    (The prohibition is binding. For the rest of the cases - considering a more liberal notion of artistic liberty, discretion shall be welcome on the part of the participants. The judges’ decision shall be final.)
    8. Pictures of members’ of competing team has to be uploaded in the Google drive along with the video.

The names of the 5 finalist teams shall be announced on or before 1st January, 2017. In certain cases, the number of finalist teams may vary by a maximum of 2 in excess and 1 in recess - depending entirely upon the discretion of the judges.
×

General Rules

  1. A debatable statement is to be presented a priori which should be duly argued for through the performance.
    (If somebody wants to argue against the statement, then the statement should be presented in negative instead.)
  2. As a part of the performance, only English shall be considered an intelligible language of communication. Bits of conversation or performance (songs, etc.) in any language other than English shall be accepted except for that the meanings of the words and usages shall be considered lost.
  3. It’s not necessary that all the members of a participating team are from the same institution. But one person may not participate in two different teams.
    Also, no further inclusion of people in a team shall be allowed following the submission for video-prelims.
×

Finale

  1. Each of the finalist teams shall be allotted 20 minutes on stage for their respective performances.
  2. It is not required that the same debatable statement argued for in the video-prelims should be argued for in the finale. However, the debatable statement that shall be argued for in the performance should be submitted in writing to the organisers before the performance begins.
  3. The statement shall be announced following the end of the performance by the usherer.

    4. (This should be noted that there is absolutely no prohibition on the team regarding stating/announcing the statement as a part of the performance.)
  4. The stage shall have a projectable screen which may be duly used by the performing team. The onus of controlling what be projected is entirely on the performing team.
    (It should be noted that the screen may be used for projecting subtitles if required. It is, however, encouraged, as has been earlier, that such usage should be minimum.)
  5. The organisers must be provided with a list of all accessory theatrical properties (and costumes, preferably) that a performing team intends to use - before 15th January, 2017. Usage of fire( including burning cigarettes), water or other items that might harm the stage is strictly prohibited. In this regard, the rules of J.N. Tata Auditorium, IISc, shall be binding and final.
  6. Nudity of primary/secondary sexual organs is strongly discouraged. Offensive usage in language and gestures is strongly discouraged. The judges’ discretion shall be final.
  7. The performances shall be judged on how well they argue for their statement and on the artistic intricacies of the performance.
    All the teams are required to remain inside the auditorium with all their members during all the performances.
    After every performance, there shall be a recess period of 5 minutes during which each member of every team (except the one that performed) shall submit individually an assessment of the last performance in terms of how well it argued for the statement they presented. (It is advisable that artistic intrigues may not be considered in this assessment.)
    After submitting their assessment forms - the team that is supposed to perform next is supposed to make ready the stage for their performance. Their performance shall be commenced once they are ready.
